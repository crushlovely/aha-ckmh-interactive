Where the two sources said the same thing, the stronger phrasing is kept; where the 2026 update added new material, it's included; where 2025 supplied unique framing (audience, tone, primary/secondary structure), it's preserved.

## Purpose

Raise patient and consumer awareness of the interconnectivity of overall health and drive care-seeking action to prevent or manage health risks. Help people understand how cardiac, kidney, and metabolic health are deeply connected, and empower them to take action early.

## Target audience

General consumer. Assumes no prior medical knowledge.

## Tone

Accessible, educational. Plain language; bulleted lists lower the reading level.

---

## Key terminology

- **CKM health** — a way to describe how your heart, kidneys, and metabolic system (how your body creates, uses, and stores energy) connect and share work. A "big picture" view of overall health.
- **CKM syndrome** — when the connected risk factors combine in ways that increase risk of heart disease and stroke. Not a single disease — several connected conditions that can build on each other over time. Divided into stages to help understand severity. Can affect a person at any age.

## Core narrative

The whole body is connected and works together like a team. Your heart, kidneys, and the way your body creates and uses energy each have different jobs, but they share the work to keep you healthy.

When one area of your health isn't working well, it can hurt the others:

- A metabolic problem like diabetes can damage your kidneys.
- Kidney problems can lead to heart disease.
- Heart disease can lead to kidney problems.

Most people don't know how closely these systems are connected. The American Heart Association is raising awareness about these connections to help people prevent, treat, and manage their overall health — focusing on the whole person, not just one disease at a time.

The biggest dangers from CKM syndrome are disability or death from heart disease and stroke (the "cardiovascular" part of CKM, which AHA has been fighting for more than 100 years). The "metabolic" part includes diabetes and obesity. Kidney disease is closely linked with both.

## Interconnected health factors

Five factors are connected to your overall health and risk for heart disease and stroke:

- Blood pressure
- Cholesterol (and other lipids)
- Weight (especially around the waist)
- Blood glucose (blood sugar)
- Kidney function

Each can worsen or improve the others. When combined, they increase the risk for heart attack, stroke, and heart failure more than any one alone. Each risk factor can increase the chance of developing another.

## Statistics (U.S. adults)

- Nearly **9 out of 10** adults (age 20+) have at least one risk factor that contributes to CKM syndrome. Even among young adults (20–44), about **8 in 10** are affected.
- Only about **10%** of U.S. adults have excellent CKM health.
- Nearly half have **high blood pressure**.
- About **1 in 3** have high total **cholesterol**; about **1 in 4** have high LDL ("bad") cholesterol; nearly **1 in 7** have low HDL ("good") cholesterol; about **1 in 5** have high triglycerides.
- More than half have **prediabetes or diabetes**.
- Over half have **high waist circumference**.
- About **1 in 7** have **kidney disease**.

## Associated conditions

Nearly every major organ system is affected by CKM syndrome. Related conditions include kidney failure, dementia, liver disease, obstructive sleep apnea, and increased cancer risk.

The greatest health threat is **cardiovascular disease**, because CKM syndrome affects blood vessels, the heart muscle's pumping ability, and the cells that control heartbeat timing. CKM syndrome is linked to greater likelihood of:

- Coronary heart disease
- Stroke
- Heart failure
- Peripheral artery disease
- Atrial fibrillation
- Cardiac arrest

## Screening

Many people don't realize they're at risk because risk factors develop slowly with few early symptoms. Regular screening helps catch heart, kidney, and metabolic problems early to prevent heart disease, stroke, and organ damage.

**Five tests for assessing CKM health:**

1. Blood pressure
2. Cholesterol panel
3. Waist circumference
4. Blood glucose (blood sugar)
5. Kidney function

If you have one CKM-related health concern, regular screening of all five components can catch additional problems early.

## Prevention & treatment

Many aspects of CKM syndrome are **treatable, preventable, and sometimes reversible**. Healthy habits and evidence-based treatments can improve multiple conditions together. Early detection plus lifestyle changes (and treatment when needed) helps prevent CKM syndrome from worsening — and can reverse it.

**Life's Essential 8** offers a proactive approach to prevention and wellness: eight key health factors critical to heart and overall health. Improving even one improves how the body functions.

The daily habits that shape CKM health over time:

- How you eat
- How you move
- How you sleep
- How you manage stress
- How you care for your body

Small changes, made consistently, add up.

## Messaging library

### Primary message — interconnectivity

- The whole body is connected. Its parts work together as a team.
- Your heart, kidneys, and the way your body creates energy work as a team. Each part has a different job, but they share the work to keep you healthy.
- When one part of the body isn't working well, it can worsen the others and raise the risk of heart disease or stroke.
- Connected health factors can worsen together and improve together.
- You can learn to make small changes today for better overall health tomorrow.
- Heart disease risk goes beyond the heart.

### Secondary message — CKM health & CKM syndrome

- A new concept for how your heart, kidneys, and energy system connect and share work is called **cardiovascular-kidney-metabolic health**, or CKM health.
- CKM health is about your overall health, not only heart health.
- CKM health is a big-picture view of your health.
- If you have risk factors for heart disease, you may have CKM syndrome.
- CKM syndrome is not a single disease. It is made up of several connected conditions that can build on each other over time.
- CKM syndrome can affect you at any age.
- CKM syndrome is divided into stages to help understand severity.
- Knowing your CKM health numbers can save your life.

### Heart–body connection

- Your heart's health is closely linked to the well-being of your entire body.
- Your body works as one connected system.
- Taking care of your heart can benefit your whole body.

### Condition / risk factor-specific

- **Weight:** CKM syndrome is primarily driven by excess weight, especially around your waist. Reaching and maintaining a healthy weight and waist size can help your heart and overall health.
- **Diabetes:** Diabetes isn't just about blood sugar — it can also impact your heart and kidneys. Keeping track of blood glucose helps protect heart, kidneys, and overall health.
- **Cholesterol/lipids:** Abnormal cholesterol and lipid levels may be an early sign of CKM syndrome.
- **Triglycerides:** The most common type of fat in the body. High levels are linked to diabetes, heart disease, and CKM syndrome.
- **Kidneys:** Monitoring kidney health is a powerful step toward protecting your heart and overall health.

## Calls to action

All CTAs drive to **heart.org/myCKMhealth** (or another relevant resource).

### Early stage (awareness, prevention)

- Strong health is built over time. Learn about small choices that can improve your heart and overall health.
- If you're working on one part of your health, other parts benefit too. Learn about the heart, kidney, and metabolic connection.
- Healthy choices and appropriate treatment can reverse or slow the progression of CKM syndrome.
- Early action helps avoid the beginning or worsening of CKM syndrome.
- If you have one component of CKM syndrome, there's a greater chance you could develop others. Learn why and what you can do.
- Regular screening of connected factors can detect CKM syndrome early.
- The conditions that lead to CKM syndrome often develop without symptoms — learn how to recognize quiet health risks.

### Later stage (management, treatment)

- Regular check-ups help see the big picture of your overall health. Schedule a visit to check on your CKM health.
- Consider a checkup of your heart, kidney, and metabolic health. Discuss screenings with your health care team.
- If you have risk factors for heart disease, ask your doctor about screening.
- CKM syndrome is manageable. Explore ways to feel better and get healthier.
- You may have options to reverse or slow CKM syndrome progression. Schedule a check-up today.
- Working together with your health care team is important for managing CKM syndrome.

### Short / standalone CTAs

- Take care of your overall health with regular checks of your blood pressure, cholesterol, weight, blood sugar, and kidney function.
- **Early detection is strong protection.** Schedule a checkup of your heart, kidney, and metabolic health.
- Learn about the heart, kidney, and metabolic connection at **heart.org/myCKMhealth**.
